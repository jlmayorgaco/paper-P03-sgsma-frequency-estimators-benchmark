\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

The benchmark is broad, but it remains finite. Its conclusions apply to the \ArchivalScenarioCount{}-scenario suite and the \ArchivalEstimatorCount{} estimators studied here; they should not be read as universal claims over every future estimator variant or every possible disturbance class.

The benchmark is also still a software benchmark. CPU time is relative desktop cost, not hardware-in-the-loop latency. The family-level statistical analysis is omnibus rather than causal or pairwise-complete. Real-time deployment claims therefore remain conditional, and a future hardware campaign would be needed before translating the present ordering directly into device-level guarantees.

Finally, the benchmark is strongest on the interaction between standard PMU-style disturbances and IBR-oriented harmonic, ringdown, and composite stress. It says less about multi-phase faults, networked estimator fusion, or plant-specific controller nonlinearities. Those are natural extension points rather than defects, but they should be stated openly.
