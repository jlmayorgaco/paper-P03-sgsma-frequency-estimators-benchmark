\section{Related Work and Algorithmic Landscape}
\label{sec:related}

Frequency estimation in low-inertia grids involves a fundamental
latency--robustness trade-off~\cite{Ting2022}. Window-based methods (IpDFT, TFT)
provide strong harmonic rejection but impose structural observation latency;
IpDFT assumes a stationary phasor over its window, whereas TFT employs a
Taylor-series model to improve RoCoF tracking at the cost of higher noise
sensitivity~\cite{PlatasGarza2011,Belega2014}. Loop-based methods [SRF-PLL, Second-Order Generalized Integrator
Frequency-Locked Loop (SOGI-FLL)] are computationally efficient but
susceptible to cycle-slip under abrupt phase
discontinuities~\cite{Golestan2015}. Adaptive recursive estimators
[Recursive Least Squares (RLS), Variable Forgetting Factor RLS (VFF-RLS)]
fit an AR(2) signal model and may diverge under severe transients.
Model-based filters (EKF, UKF, RA-EKF) offer an improved
latency--robustness balance~\cite{Ferrero2020EKF,Chamorro2021};
the RA-EKF adds Innovation-Driven Covariance Scaling and Event Gating
to address IBR-specific islanding transients. Data-driven approaches
(Koopman, PI-GRU) achieve competitive accuracy in some regimes but differ
substantially in computational cost~\cite{Ting2022,Zelaya2025}. Prior studies
typically use fixed tuning parameters and evaluate disturbances in isolation;
combined phase discontinuities, sustained RoCoF, and harmonic distortion are
not assessed simultaneously. Table~\ref{tab:unified_2024_big}
provides a qualitative comparison of eleven of the twelve evaluated estimators.
