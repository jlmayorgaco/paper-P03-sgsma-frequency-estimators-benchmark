than SRF-PLL and $1.41\!\times$ lower than EKF.

\subsection{Robustness Under Severe IBR Disturbances}
% ---------------------------------------------------------

\subsubsection{Composite Islanding (Scenario D)}

Window-based methods exhibit spectral leakage: IpDFT reaches RMSE\,$=484$\,mHz (peak $2910$\,mHz); TFT peak $2740$\,mHz.
EKF/UKF, lacking phase-reset mechanisms, incur $T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0.122$\,s; SRF-PLL avoids leakage (RMSE\,$=45.0$\,mHz, $T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0$).
Among model-based estimators, the RA-EKF achieves RMSE\,$=39.3$\,mHz---a $5.7\!\times$ RMSE reduction vs.\ EKF---with zero threshold breaches ($T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0$; peak $365$\,mHz $<0.5$\,Hz, Fig.~\ref{fig:dashboard_mega}g).
\emph{Note:} Scenario~D applies all disturbances simultaneously; isolating
the contribution of harmonic leakage vs.\ noise amplification requires
single-effect experiments outside this benchmark's scope.

\subsubsection{Multi-Event Stress Test (Scenario E)}

Table~\ref{tab:risk_metrics} quantifies trip-risk under the IBR Multi-Event sequence (5th/7th harmonics; THD\,$\approx$\,4.5\%, IEEE~519 compliant). SOGI-FLL incurs the highest trip-risk ($3.10$\,s); SRF-PLL accumulates $T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0.353$\,s (RMSE\,$=307$\,mHz). The RA-EKF achieves RMSE\,$=159$\,mHz with $T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0.081$\,s at $88.5\,\mu$s/sample---a $4.4\!\times$ trip-risk reduction vs.\ SRF-PLL and $4.5\!\times$ vs.\ EKF in this benchmark. IpDFT: $T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0.563$\,s; Koopman: $0.068$\,s.

\subsection{Computational Feasibility}
% ---------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:complexity} and Fig.~\ref{fig:dashboard_mega}e show the
cost--risk Pareto frontier. All timings are Python~3.13 baselines
(Intel Core Ultra 5 125H, single-threaded) for \emph{relative comparison
only}; C/FPGA implementations yield 10--100$\times$ lower absolute latencies.
SRF-PLL ($16.7\,\mu$s) and EKF ($29.5\,\mu$s) are least expensive; SRF-PLL
incurs elevated trip-risk ($T_{\mathrm{trip}}\!=\!0.353$\,s, Scenario~E).
RA-EKF ($88.5\,\mu$s) is a viable DSP/FPGA candidate pending C/HIL validation.
PI-GRU (${\approx}154$\,ms/sample) is prohibitive for embedded real-time
protection without GPU acceleration.

\subsection{Failure Mode Summary by Estimator Family}
% ---------------------------------------------------------

Dominant failure modes (Table~\ref{tab:unified_2024_big}):
\textbf{Window-based:} spectral leakage ($>450$\,mHz RMSE, Scenario~D) and structural latency.
\textbf{Loop-based:} SRF-PLL cycle-slip under phase jumps;
SOGI-FLL type-1 loop fails sustained RoCoF ($1120$\,mHz, Scenario~B).
\textbf{Adaptive recursive:} VFF blow-up and covariance instability under multi-event
transients; TKEO diverges under noise (RMSE\,$\geq$\,7800\,mHz, excluded from Tables~II--III).
\textbf{Data-driven:} PI-GRU dataset shift---the fixed pre-trained model underperforms
scenario-optimized classical methods on RMSE and trip-risk in all five scenarios.
\textbf{Model-based:} EKF/UKF Jacobian divergence ($T_{\mathrm{trip}}\!=\!0.120$\,s,
Scenario~D); RA-EKF event-gating mitigates this.
