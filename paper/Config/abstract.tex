\begin{abstract}
In low-inertia grids dominated by Inverter-Based Resources (IBRs), the latency and robustness of frequency estimation are critical for protection and dynamic security assessment. This paper evaluates twelve estimators from five algorithmic families under five stress scenarios using dual-rate numerical simulation (1\,MHz physics / 10\,kHz DSP). Each estimator is independently tuned by scenario-wise grid search and assessed on RMSE, peak error, trip-risk duration ($T_{\mathrm{trip}}$), and computational cost. Tests (A)--(C) follow the IEC/IEEE~60255-118-1 test philosophy; Scenarios (D)--(E) introduce IBR-specific phase discontinuities and harmonic distortion beyond the standard scope. Under a sustained frequency ramp ($+3$\,Hz/s), the RoCoF-Augmented Extended Kalman Filter (RA-EKF) achieves RMSE\,$=0.00823$\,Hz—$8.15\times$ lower than SRF-PLL. Under composite islanding, the RA-EKF incurs zero threshold breaches across all $N\!=\!30$ Monte Carlo trials ($T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0$), versus $T_{\mathrm{trip}}=0.120$\,s for the standard EKF. Window-based and loop-based methods exhibit complementary strengths across scenario types. In the evaluated scenarios, data-driven approaches exhibited higher computational cost and did not demonstrate consistent accuracy gains over scenario-optimized classical methods. The findings indicate that several estimators passing IEC/IEEE~60255-118-1 standard tests degrade under composite disturbances, suggesting that the standard test scope may not fully characterize estimator behavior in IBR-dominated conditions.
\end{abstract}
