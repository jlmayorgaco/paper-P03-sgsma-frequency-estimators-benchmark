\subsection{Algorithmic Landscape and Prior Art}
\label{sec:related}

Frequency estimation involves a latency--robustness trade-off~\cite{Ting2022}.
\emph{Structural observation latency} ($N_c/f_0$ for window-based methods) is
signal-independent; \emph{stochastic estimation lag} depends on disturbance type
and estimator parameters---these are independent metrics.
Window-based methods (IpDFT, TFT) provide harmonic rejection but impose structural
latency~\cite{PlatasGarza2011,Belega2014}. Loop-based methods (SRF-PLL, SOGI-FLL)
are computationally efficient but susceptible to cycle-slip under phase
discontinuities~\cite{Golestan2015}. Adaptive recursive estimators (RLS, VFF-RLS)
may diverge under severe transients. Model-based filters (EKF, UKF, RA-EKF)
offer improved tracking~\cite{Ferrero2020EKF,Chamorro2021}. Data-driven approaches
(Koopman, PI-GRU) show competitive accuracy at higher cost~\cite{Ting2022,Zelaya2025}.
Prior studies evaluate disturbances in isolation; composite scenarios are not assessed
simultaneously. Table~\ref{tab:unified_2024_big} compares all twelve evaluated estimators.
